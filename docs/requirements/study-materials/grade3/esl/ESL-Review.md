# ESL Review - Grammar Knowledge

## 1. Tag Questions
**Use:**
* to check information
* to ask for agreement

**Form:**
* Positive sentence → negative tag
* Negative sentence → positive tag

**Examples:**
* You are tired, aren’t you?
* She isn’t here, is she?

## 2. Would You Like (Offers & Invitations)
**Use:**
* polite offers
* polite invitations

**Forms:**
* Offer: Would you like + noun
* Invitation: Would you like + to + verb

**Examples:**
* Would you like some juice?
* Would you like to come with us?

**Responses:**
* Accept: Yes, please / Yes, I’d love to
* Refuse: No, thank you / Sorry, I can’t

## 3. Present Simple
**Use:**
* routines
* habits
* general facts

**Forms:**
* I/You/We/They + V
* He/She/It + V-s/es
* Negative: S + do/does not + V
* Question: Do/Does + S + V?

**Examples:**
* She goes to school every day.
* He doesn’t like milk.

**Signal Words:** always, usually, often, sometimes, every day

## 4. Present Continuous (Future Meaning)
**Use:** planned future arrangements
**Form:** S + am/is/are + V-ing
**Examples:** I am meeting my teacher tomorrow.
**Time Expressions:** tomorrow, tonight, next week

## 5. Past Continuous
**Use:**
* action in progress in the past
* background action

**Form:** S + was/were + V-ing
**Examples:** I was studying when she called.
**Signal Words:** when, while

## 6. Present Perfect
**Use:**
* experiences
* actions with no exact time

**Form:** S + have/has + V3
**Examples:**
* I have finished my homework.
* She has visited Hanoi.

**Signal Words:** already, just, yet, ever, never

## 7. Future Forms
**Will**
* Form: S + will + V
* Use: predictions, instant decisions
* Example: I think it will rain.

**Be Going To**
* Form: S + am/is/are + going to + V
* Use: planned actions, predictions with evidence
* Example: I am going to travel this weekend.

## 8. Obligation & Possibility
**Obligation**
* Form: S + have/has to/had to + V
* Example: I have to do my homework.

**Possibility**
* Form: S + might / may / could + V
* Example: She might come later.

## 9. Defining Relative Clauses
**Use:** give extra information about a noun
**Words:**
* who → people
* which → things
* that → people/things
* where → places

**Structure:** Noun + who/which/that/where + clause
**Example:** The boy who is running is my brother.

## 10. Like & About
**Like:** used for similarity
→ She looks like her mother.

**About:** used for topic
→ We are talking about animals.

## 11. Prepositions of Direction
**Use:** show movement
**Common Prepositions:**
* into (movement inside)
* out of (movement outside)
* from (starting point)
* towards (in the direction of)

**Examples:** The cat ran into the room.
